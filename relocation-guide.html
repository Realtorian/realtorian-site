The big picture

Why People Are Moving Here

South Carolina ranked among the top states for net domestic migration in 2025 — more people moving in than leaving. Upstate SC is driving much of that. Newcomers are arriving from Florida, New York, California, Georgia, and North Carolina. Here's what's drawing them.

💰
Affordability That's Still Real
Housing costs run about 27% below the national average. You can still find new construction in quality school zones for what a starter condo costs in Atlanta or Charlotte. The secret is getting out — but it's getting out fast.
🏔️
Nature at Your Doorstep
The Blue Ridge foothills start right in your backyard. Lakes, rivers, waterfalls, and hundreds of miles of trails — within 30 minutes of most Upstate communities. This isn't "near the mountains." It's in them.
🏙️
City Amenities, Small-Town Feel
Greenville's Main Street is legitimately one of the best downtowns in the South — walkable, vibrant, full of restaurants, breweries, and live music. But you're still 10 minutes from quiet neighborhoods and farmland.
💼
A Growing Job Market
BMW, Michelin, Fluor, Prisma Health, and a wave of tech and logistics companies have planted roots here. The Upstate SC Alliance announced $1.2B+ in capital investment and thousands of new jobs in recent years.
🎓
Strong Schools
More than half of South Carolina's Top 20 school districts are in the Upstate. Greenville County is the largest district in the state — and routinely ranks at the top. Clemson and Furman are right in your backyard.
☀️
Four Seasons, Mild Winters
Warm summers, brilliant fall color, and winters mild enough that snow is a novelty rather than a burden. You get a real autumn without shoveling a driveway for three months. That alone sells a lot of Northerners.
🏥
Excellent Healthcare
Prisma Health is the largest system in SC. Bon Secours St. Francis, Spartanburg Regional, and AnMed round out a deep regional network. Three Upstate hospitals rank in the state's Top 10 by US News.
🧾
Retiree-Friendly Taxes
SC doesn't tax Social Security. Retirement deductions up to $15,000 per spouse. Homestead exemptions can reduce property taxes to a few hundred dollars a year for seniors. Florida isn't the only option anymore.
🌐
Remote-Work Ready
Reliable fiber internet in most communities. Lower cost of living means your remote salary goes further. Growing co-working scene in Greenville and Spartanburg. More and more remote workers are choosing the Upcountry over expensive coastal metros.

Four real seasons

Climate & Weather

The Upstate sits at 800–1,000 feet elevation in the foothills of the Blue Ridge. You get four distinct seasons — including brilliant fall color and mild winters — without the brutal cold of the Northeast or the year-round heat of the coast.

Spring
🌸
55–75°F
Gorgeous. Mild temps, dogwoods blooming, waterfalls running full. The best season for outdoor exploration.
Summer
☀️
80–95°F
Hot and humid July–August. Lakes save you. A/C is non-negotiable. Evenings cool down nicely in the foothills.
Fall
🍂
50–72°F
Arguably the best fall color in the Southeast outside of western NC. Crisp air, perfect for hiking and drives.
Winter
❄️
32–55°F
Mild. Snow is a novelty — maybe 1–2 events per year. Ice storms happen occasionally. No months of shoveling.

Your move, simplified

Relocation Checklist

Moving from out of state? Here's a high-level checklist to keep your relocation on track. David can help with the real estate piece — and point you toward the right resources for everything else.

1
Determine your target county/community based on schools, commute, and budget
2
Get pre-approved with a local SC lender familiar with the market
3
Schedule a visit to walk neighborhoods in person — weekday and weekend
4
Research school district boundaries before making an offer — they don't always match city lines
5
Understand HOA rules and annual fees for any subdivision you're considering
6
Get a home inspection — always, even on new construction in SC
7
Transfer vehicle registration within 45 days of establishing SC residency
8
Apply for SC driver's license within 90 days of moving
9
Register to vote at your new address
10
Apply for SC homestead exemption if over 65 or disabled — major property tax savings
11
Set up utilities: Duke Energy (electric), local water provider, internet (AT&T Fiber or Spectrum in most areas)
12
Find a local closing attorney — SC is an attorney-close state, not a title company state
